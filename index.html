install app

◆ iphone & ipad — safari

  1. tap the share icon (the square with an arrow)
  2. scroll down and choose add to home screen
  3. tap add — tally lands on your home screen

◆ android — chrome

  1. tap the ⋮ menu (top right)
  2. choose install app or add to home screen
  3. confirm to add it

once installed it opens full-screen and works offline.

about

tally — a quiet training log. no accounts, no cloud, no upsells. just you and the work.

how tally works

logging

a session is everything you log on one date. each entry in it is one exercise, filed under a category. strength entries hold sets — reps × weight, in lb; cardio entries hold a distance and/or a time. change a session's date to move it in the log.

there are four categories, each with its own colour: cardio, core, upper body, and lower body.

volume — your strength work

volume is the total weight you moved:

volume = sum of (reps × weight)

added up across every set in view. a set missing its reps or its weight is skipped. it's measured in lb and always drawn in lime.

cardio — your conditioning work

a distance is read as kilometres (miles are converted, ×1.609); a time is read as minutes, and it accepts plain 45, 28 min, 1 h, or 1:05:00. your cardio score is simply how far you went, in feet:

from distance: ft = km × 3281
from time: ft = min × 547  (a 6 min/km pace)

if you log both distance and time, the two are averaged; if you log only one, that one stands alone. a time on its own is scored as the distance you'd cover at a steady 6 min/km pace. it's always drawn in blue.

the log tab

every session, newest first — each showing its volume and its cardio score, the exercise and set counts, and a coloured dot for every category you touched that day.

the dashboard — home

the switch up top (all time · 30 days · 7 days) filters everything below it to that window, counting back from today.

top stats — total volume in the window, number of sessions (distinct days trained), average volume per session, and total sets.

cardio — your total cardio score over the window and how many cardio sessions made it up.

volume + cardio chart — your last 8 sessions, oldest to newest. each bar stacks volume (lime) beneath cardio (blue): its full height is that day's whole training load, and the split shows where the load came from. the bars scale to your biggest day.

exercises by category — how many entries fell into each category, the bar showing that category's share of your busiest one.

records — the single heaviest set ever logged for each exercise, with the day you first hit it, grouped by category. tap to expand.

supplements

logged per day on the supps tab, then summarised on the dashboard: every supplement you've ever logged, with how many separate days you took it and the running total (shown in whichever unit you use most for it), ranked by days taken.

numbers & storage

any figure of 1,000 or more is shortened with a k — 1,500 shows as 1.5k. and everything stays on this device, in the browser's own database: no server ever sees it. export a json backup whenever you like, and import it to restore.
